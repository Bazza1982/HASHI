# AAAJ-Style SLR Writing Template

This template is extracted from a sample AAAJ-style systematic literature
review draft. It captures structure and paragraph function only. Replace all
placeholders with the new paper's topic, theory, dataset, and findings.

## Article Type

Systematic literature review with a theory-led synthesis.

Core pattern:

```text
field evolution -> fragmented literature -> theoretical lens -> systematic
review method -> mapped evidence -> thematic synthesis -> theory-building
discussion -> concise conclusion
```

## Front Matter

### Title

Use a two-part conceptual title.

Function:

- first part names the conceptual movement or contrast
- second part names the article type and empirical/literature domain

Template:

```text
From [old conceptual frame] to [new conceptual frame]:
A systematic review of [field/domain] literature ([date range])
```

### Structured Abstract

Use short labelled moves.

#### Purpose

One paragraph, about 40-70 words.

Function:

- identify the field or phenomenon
- state why the literature is fragmented or incomplete
- state what the review aims to integrate or explain

#### Design/methodology/approach

One paragraph, about 30-50 words.

Function:

- state corpus size and date range
- state journal/sample boundary
- name the review methods
- name the theoretical or analytical lens

#### Findings

One paragraph, about 50-80 words.

Function:

- summarise the main developmental shift
- name the major themes or patterns
- state what the field now prioritises or neglects

#### Research limitations/implications

One paragraph, about 20-40 words.

Function:

- state what the review reveals as blind spots
- point to future empirical or theoretical work

#### Originality/value

One paragraph, about 35-60 words.

Function:

- claim what is new about the synthesis
- name the conceptual contribution
- explain how the review reframes the field

#### Keywords

Use 5-8 terms:

```text
[domain]; systematic literature review; [core theory]; [subfield 1];
[subfield 2]; [emerging topic]
```

#### Paper type

Use:

```text
Literature Review
```

## 1. Introduction

Approximate length: 10-12 substantive paragraphs plus research questions.

### Paragraph 1: Field-level opening

Function:

- open with a broad historical or institutional shift
- show that the phenomenon has expanded beyond its original domain
- cite foundational and recent sources

Language move:

```text
Over the past [period], [phenomenon] has moved beyond [old domain] and become
a broader mode of [governance/verification/knowledge production].
```

### Paragraph 2: Existing empirical literature

Function:

- show that a substantial literature already exists
- identify what that literature has consistently examined
- establish credibility before claiming a gap

### Paragraph 3: Fragmentation/gap

Function:

- explain how existing reviews or syntheses are too narrow
- identify the subdomains that have been studied separately
- state what remains unsynthesised

### Paragraph 4: Why the gap matters

Function:

- explain the consequence of fragmentation
- show what is hidden when subdomains are studied in isolation
- make the gap theoretically or practically important

### Paragraph 5: Theoretical lens

Function:

- introduce the core theory or conceptual frame
- explain why it is useful for the review
- show how the theory has evolved or been refined

### Paragraph 6: Underused theory/gap in engagement

Function:

- show that the theory is relevant but unevenly used
- connect the theoretical gap to the review's purpose

### Paragraph 7: Review warrant

Function:

- state why a systematic re-examination is warranted now
- connect time period, literature development, and theoretical need

### Research Questions

Use two research questions.

RQ1 should map field development:

```text
How has the empirical literature on [phenomenon] developed over [period]?
```

RQ2 should connect the field to the theoretical lens:

```text
To what extent does the literature engage with [theory/refinement], and what
does this reveal about [core conceptual issue]?
```

### Paragraph after RQs: Scope and definition

Function:

- define the review object broadly enough to integrate subdomains
- state inclusion boundaries
- justify the definition

### Contribution paragraph

Function:

- present 2-3 contributions
- begin with a mapping/synthesis contribution
- then state conceptual/theoretical contribution
- then state field-building or future-research contribution

### Roadmap paragraph

Function:

- state what each section does
- keep it concise and sequential

## 2. Conceptual Foundations

Approximate length: 8-9 paragraphs.

Purpose:

- establish the theoretical frame before the review method
- show the evolution of the theory
- create concepts that will later organise the synthesis

Paragraph sequence:

1. Introduce the main theorist/concept and why it matters.
2. Explain the earliest conceptual building block.
3. Give a concrete example of how the concept operates in practice.
4. Explain how the original theory generalised beyond the initial domain.
5. Introduce later theoretical refinement 1.
6. Introduce later theoretical refinement 2.
7. Clarify how related terms should be understood together.
8. Link the theory to the empirical review domain.

Language style:

- dense but explanatory
- theory-led, not purely descriptive
- frequent use of "while", "however", "subsequently", "in this sense"

## 3. Review Design and Methodology

Purpose:

- show that the review is systematic, transparent, and theoretically guided
- justify the corpus
- explain search, screening, and analysis

### Opening methodology paragraph

Function:

- summarise the full review design
- name the stages of the method
- explain why multiple methods are combined

### Systematic Literature Review subsection

Paragraph sequence:

1. Justify standalone literature review as a rigorous method.
2. Explain quality principles such as rigour, scope, generativity, and
   transparency.
3. Present search domains and keywords, often supported by a table.
4. Describe databases, date range, journal boundary, and inclusion criteria.
5. Explain screening, filtering, and final corpus construction.
6. Use tables/figures to make selection transparent.

Template table roles:

- Table 1: search terms or subject domains
- Table 2: journal list or quality boundary
- Figure 1: selection protocol or PRISMA-style flow

### Corpus description

Function:

- describe the final dataset
- explain why it is adequate for the review purpose
- introduce descriptive patterns before deeper synthesis

## 4. Analysis and Thematic Synthesis

Purpose:

- move from corpus construction to conceptual interpretation
- combine bibliometric/descriptive mapping with inductive thematic synthesis

### Analytical framework and thematic development

Paragraph sequence:

1. Introduce the analytical framework.
2. Explain why the framework fits the review object.
3. Link keyword clusters or evidence patterns to framework dimensions.
4. Define each dimension briefly.
5. Use a mapping table to connect evidence clusters to themes.

### Meta-synthesis subsection

Function:

- turn descriptive patterns into interpretive themes
- show how empirical findings deepen or complicate the theory
- use a synthesis table to present themes, evidence, and implications

### Theme subsections

Use 4-6 theme subsections.

Each theme subsection should follow this paragraph rhythm:

1. Topic sentence naming the theme and why it matters.
2. Evidence paragraph summarising what the literature shows.
3. Interpretation paragraph linking evidence to the theoretical frame.
4. Tension/gap paragraph explaining what remains unresolved.
5. Transition sentence connecting to the next theme.

Possible theme heading pattern:

```text
[Theme as conceptual claim, not just topic label]
```

## 5. Discussion and Conclusion

Purpose:

- return to the research questions
- explain what the synthesis changes conceptually
- identify theoretical implications and future agenda

### Opening discussion subsection

Function:

- restate the field transformation
- answer RQ1 at a high level
- explain the qualitative shift shown by the review

### Conceptual discussion subsections

Use 4-5 subsections.

Each subsection should:

1. Name a conceptual insight from the synthesis.
2. Connect it to the theoretical lens.
3. Show how the reviewed literature supports or extends it.
4. Explain what this means for the field.

Language moves:

- "The review shows that..."
- "This suggests that..."
- "Viewed through [theory], ..."
- "Rather than..., the evidence indicates..."

### Final conceptual synthesis subsection

Function:

- introduce the article's strongest conceptual phrase or framing
- explain how it extends the existing theory
- make the paper memorable

## 6. Conclusion

Approximate length: 5 paragraphs.

Paragraph sequence:

1. Restate the review purpose and answer RQ1.
2. Answer RQ2 and connect back to the theory.
3. Identify unevenness or limitations in the literature.
4. State review limitations transparently.
5. Provide future research agenda and closing contribution.

Style:

- concise
- integrative
- no new evidence
- returns to the title/conceptual framing

## References

Use the target journal reference style consistently.

Function:

- demonstrate field coverage
- support the SLR's authority
- include foundational theory, method papers, and empirical corpus sources

## Language Style Guide

Overall voice:

- formal, theory-led, and synthesis-oriented
- long but controlled paragraphs
- frequent contrast and refinement moves
- field-level claims supported by dense citation clusters

Common sentence functions:

- establish historical development
- identify fragmentation
- explain why fragmentation matters
- introduce theory as a lens
- state systematic review design
- transform descriptive findings into conceptual claims
- return to research questions in discussion

Avoid:

- generic "this paper is about" phrasing
- unsupported claims of novelty
- descriptive literature summary without synthesis
- theme headings that are only topic labels
- copying source article language
