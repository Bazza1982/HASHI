# AAAJ SLR Writing Template v2

Source type: published systematic literature review PDF.

Use this template to write a new SLR article with the same rhetorical structure,
not the same content. Each paragraph prompt names the action the paragraph must
perform.

## Article Logic

```text
field emergence
-> conceptual contrast
-> review warrant
-> theoretical antecedents
-> systematic review design
-> exploratory/descriptive mapping
-> narrative synthesis by phases
-> research agenda
-> conceptual warning
```

## Title

Formula:

```text
Unpacking [concept/field]:
a systematic literature review and research agenda
```

Use when the article aims to open up a field, organise past work, and build a
future agenda.

## Structured Abstract

### Purpose

Write one paragraph that:

1. names the literature field
2. states the review aim
3. says what themes or developments the review examines
4. signals future agenda

### Design/methodology/approach

Write one paragraph that:

1. states the review is systematic
2. gives corpus size
3. names databases/search sources
4. states date range
5. states source boundary

### Findings

Write one paragraph with three layers:

1. classification or content analysis output
2. bibliometric/descriptive mapping output
3. narrative review/conceptual development output

### Originality/value

Write one paragraph that:

1. states what the review synthesises
2. states what agenda or conceptual extension it creates
3. explains why this matters to the field

## 1. Introduction

Target: about 8 paragraphs plus a roadmap.

### P1 Field emergence

Purpose:

- introduce the reviewed field as recent/productive/significant
- make the topic matter to the discipline

Prompt:

```text
[Field] has become increasingly important in [discipline] because [practice/problem/tension].
```

### P2 Practical/conceptual rationale

Purpose:

- explain the practice problem the field responds to
- connect the field to stakeholders, governance, accountability, or knowledge production

Prompt:

```text
This development reflects a need to rethink [dominant practice] in light of [broader concern].
```

### P3 Representative approaches

Purpose:

- show the field contains multiple approaches/tools/methods
- demonstrate breadth without becoming a full review

Prompt:

```text
Prior studies have proposed approaches such as [approach 1], [approach 2], and [approach 3].
```

### P4 Main conceptual contrast

Purpose:

- define the reviewed concept against a dominant alternative
- clarify stakes

Prompt:

```text
Whereas [dominant approach] assumes [assumption], [reviewed concept] foregrounds [alternative logic].
```

### P5 Positive definition

Purpose:

- explain what the concept enables
- move from contrast to constructive definition

Prompt:

```text
From this perspective, [concept] enables [function], [function], and [function].
```

### P6 Research questions

Purpose:

- turn the field into review questions

Prompts:

```text
RQ1. What has been done in the literature on [topic]?
RQ2. What could be done to advance research on [topic]?
```

### P7 Review purposes

Purpose:

- state 2-3 purposes linked to the method

Prompt:

```text
The review has a [twofold/threefold] purpose: first, [understand/map]; second, [cluster/classify]; third, [develop agenda].
```

### P8 Roadmap

Purpose:

- tell readers what each section does

Prompt:

```text
The article proceeds as follows. Section 2..., Section 3..., Section 4..., Section 5...
```

## 2. Antecedents / Theoretical Background

Target: about 5 paragraphs.

### P1 Intellectual ancestry

Name the theories/traditions that shaped the field.

### P2 Foundational tradition 1

Explain the first tradition's core idea and why it matters.

### P3 Foundational tradition 2

Explain the second tradition and how it adds another dimension.

### P4 Foundational tradition 3

Explain the debate, tension, or pluralism that makes the field theoretically rich.

### P5 Transition to method

Close by saying these premises motivate the review design.

## 3. Review Design and Exploratory Analyses

Target: method transparency plus field mapping.

### P1 Method overview

Justify method pluralism:

```text
SLR defines the field boundary, while [content analysis/bibliometrics/narrative review] adds interpretive depth.
```

### 3.1 Systematic Literature Review

Write paragraphs for:

1. why SLR is appropriate
2. databases/search tools
3. search string
4. inclusion criteria
5. screening and deduplication
6. final corpus size and time period
7. publication trend or selection figure

### 3.2 Content Analysis

Write paragraphs for:

1. why each contribution is coded
2. coding categories
3. theoretical vs empirical classification
4. methods/geography/sector profile
5. table summarising classification

### 3.3 Bibliometric Visualisations

Write paragraphs for:

1. why visualisation helps with a large corpus
2. bibliographic coupling
3. keyword cooccurrence
4. coauthorship or journal coupling
5. what each figure reveals

## 4. Narrative Review and Discussion of Contributions

Target: transform the corpus into a developmental story.

### P1 Narrative rationale

Explain why influential/high-quality/innovative works are discussed in depth.

### P2 Chronological or thematic roadmap

State the phases or strands that organise the narrative.

### Phase subsection pattern

For each phase:

1. Phase opening: period, number/type of works, why it matters.
2. Key contribution cluster: discuss representative works together.
3. Conceptual implication: explain what the cluster adds to the field.
4. Tension/gap: identify what remains unresolved.
5. Transition: explain how this phase leads to the next.

Use 3-4 phases:

```text
4.1 Seminal/theoretical foundations
4.2 Maturity and core debates
4.3 Emerging topics and contexts
4.4 Innovative methodologies and future-facing applications
```

## 5. Discussion and Conclusions

Target: convert review into research agenda.

### P1 Aim restatement

Return to the purpose and evidence base.

### P2 Field trajectory synthesis

Condense the phases into a single field-level story.

### P3 Future agenda direction 1

Name one future area and explain why the review points there.

### P4 Future agenda direction 2

Name a contextual/institutional issue and explain its importance.

### P5 Conceptual warning

End by warning what would be lost if the concept becomes a mere technique,
label, or legitimation device.

## Tables, Figures, Appendix

Use evidence devices with explicit functions:

- corpus trend figure: shows field growth
- classification table: makes corpus comparable
- bibliometric network: shows intellectual structure
- keyword map: shows thematic clustering
- appendix: supports transparency

## Human/AI Use Checklist

Before drafting:

- Can the writer explain the source article's argument in one sentence?
- Are the new topic's theoretical antecedents identified?
- Are RQ1 and RQ2 broad enough for a review?
- Are methods linked to the review purpose?
- Is the narrative review organised by phases or strands?
- Does the discussion produce an agenda rather than repeat findings?
- Are all source-specific claims removed?

## Do Not

- copy the source topic or findings
- copy the source phase labels unless the new field genuinely has the same phases
- treat bibliometrics as a substitute for narrative synthesis
- write a generic literature review without conceptual agenda
