# Published AAAJ SLR PDF-Derived Writing Template

Source: published PDF article, "Unpacking dialogic accounting: a systematic literature review and research agenda".

Purpose: provide a reusable writing structure for a systematic literature review article. This template captures article architecture and paragraph function only. Replace all content with the new topic, corpus, theory, and findings.

## Overall Article Logic

```text
emerging field/problem
-> conceptual contrast
-> review questions/purpose
-> theoretical antecedents
-> systematic review design
-> descriptive and bibliometric mapping
-> narrative review of contributions
-> research agenda and conclusion
```

## Front Matter

### Title

Use a direct title naming:

- the object being unpacked
- the article type
- the forward-looking contribution

Template:

```text
Unpacking [concept/field]:
a systematic literature review and research agenda
```

### Structured Abstract

Use labelled AAAJ-style abstract moves.

#### Purpose

One paragraph.

Function:

- state the article's review purpose
- name the literature field
- say what the review examines
- indicate future development or research agenda

Template:

```text
This article aims to contribute to [literature/field] by reviewing how previous studies have addressed [topic], examining the main themes investigated, and discussing potential further developments of the research agenda.
```

#### Design/methodology/approach

One paragraph.

Function:

- state the review type
- give corpus size
- name databases/sources
- state date range
- state outlet/source boundary

Template:

```text
The study builds on a systematic literature review of [number] research products indexed in [databases] and published between [years] in [journal/book/source boundary].
```

#### Findings

One paragraph, multi-part.

Function:

- first state classification output
- then state bibliometric/descriptive output
- then state narrative synthesis output

Template:

```text
First, [analysis type] informs a classification in terms of [categories]. Second, [mapping/visualisation method] provides representations of [network/field structure]. Third, the narrative review discusses the development of [field] from [early phase], through [core phase], to [recent/emerging phase].
```

#### Originality/value

One paragraph.

Function:

- claim the review's distinctive contribution
- link retrospective synthesis with future agenda

Template:

```text
The contribution is twofold: it provides a systematic and narrative review of the evolution of [field], and it develops ideas for future research by discussing how [emerging conditions] may shape the agenda.
```

#### Keywords

Use 5-7 terms:

```text
[core concept], Literature review, [theoretical field], [subfield], Bibliometric analysis, [related domain]
```

#### Paper type

Use:

```text
Research paper
```

## 1. Introduction

Approximate function: open the field, define the conceptual contrast, explain why a review is needed, state review questions/purpose, and preview the paper.

### Paragraph Cluster 1: Field emergence

Role:

- introduce the topic as a recent but productive field
- state that the concept responds to limitations in existing practices
- make the topic consequential for the discipline

Write:

```text
[Topic] has emerged as a productive field of study within [discipline]. Its development reflects growing recognition that [central problem/tension] requires rethinking [practice/concept/tool].
```

### Paragraph Cluster 2: Core conceptual contrast

Role:

- define the main concept by contrasting it with an opposing concept
- explain why the contrast matters
- make the reader understand the stakes before the review begins

Write:

```text
Whereas [dominant/monologic approach] assumes [assumption], [alternative/dialogic approach] foregrounds [plurality/participation/conflict/context].
```

### Paragraph Cluster 3: Concept elaboration through prior literature

Role:

- show that prior scholarship has proposed tools, methods, or approaches
- give examples of the concept's practical or theoretical variants
- avoid reviewing everything; only clarify the field's shape

### Paragraph Cluster 4: Research warrant and questions

Role:

- state that a systematic review is needed
- frame the article around broad review questions

Template:

```text
This study conducts a systematic literature review to answer two fundamental questions: What has been done? What could be done?
```

Adaptation:

```text
RQ1. What has the literature on [topic] done so far?
RQ2. What future directions, gaps, or agenda emerge from this literature?
```

### Paragraph Cluster 5: Review purpose

Role:

- present two or three purposes
- move from understanding the field to clustering contributions and setting an agenda

Template:

```text
The review has a [twofold/threefold] purpose: first, to understand [insights/contributions]; second, to cluster [themes/methods/topics]; and third, to identify [future research agenda].
```

### Paragraph Cluster 6: Roadmap

Role:

- preview each section in order
- connect method, narrative review, and conclusion

## 2. Antecedents / Theoretical Background

Approximate function: explain the intellectual roots that make the reviewed field understandable.

### Paragraph Cluster 1: Name the intellectual ancestors

Role:

- identify the theories/traditions that shaped the field
- explain why they appear before the review method

Template:

```text
Among the studies and traditions that most inspired research on [topic] are [tradition 1], [tradition 2], and [tradition 3].
```

### Paragraph Clusters 2-N: Explain each theoretical ancestor

For each tradition:

- name the core idea
- explain its relevance to the reviewed topic
- show how it influenced later studies

Paragraph function:

```text
[Theory/tradition] matters because it provides [conceptual resource]. This helps explain why later studies approach [topic] as [field-specific implication].
```

### Final paragraph: Transition to review design

Role:

- conclude that the theoretical background motivates the review
- hand off to methodology

Template:

```text
In light of these theoretical premises, the following sections illustrate the methodology and main results of the literature review.
```

## 3. Review Design and Exploratory Analyses

Approximate function: make the review systematic, transparent, and analytically rich.

### Opening paragraph

Role:

- explain why SLR defines the field boundary
- explain why additional methods are needed
- preview subsections

Template:

```text
Against this multifaceted background, the review first uses a systematic literature review to define the domain. To overcome the limitations of SLR alone, it then combines [content analysis/bibliometric visualisation/narrative discussion].
```

### 3.1 Systematic Literature Review

Paragraph sequence:

1. Justify literature analysis as a method.
2. State databases and search string.
3. State selection parameters:
   - language
   - source type
   - research domain
   - topic relevance
4. Explain screening and deduplication.
5. State final corpus size and time period.
6. Use a figure/table to show publication trend or selection flow.

### 3.2 Content Analysis

Paragraph sequence:

1. Explain why content analysis is the next step.
2. Define coding categories.
3. Separate theoretical and empirical contributions.
4. Classify methods, geography, sector, findings, conclusions, and future research.
5. Use a table to summarise corpus classifications.

Table function:

```text
Table: Classification of research products by methodology, geography, and sector.
```

### 3.3 Bibliometric Visualisations

Paragraph sequence:

1. Justify bibliometric analysis for a large corpus.
2. Explain bibliographic coupling.
3. Explain cooccurrence analysis.
4. Explain coauthorship or journal coupling if relevant.
5. Use figures to show intellectual networks.

Figure functions:

- publication trend
- bibliographic network
- keyword cooccurrence
- coauthorship map
- journal coupling map

## 4. Narrative Review and Discussion of Contributions

Approximate function: transform systematic evidence into a historical and conceptual narrative.

### Opening paragraph

Role:

- explain why not every article is discussed equally
- justify focusing on influential, high-quality, or innovative contributions
- link narrative review to bibliometric/descriptive findings

Template:

```text
Given the breadth of the corpus, this section discusses the most relevant works in terms of contribution quality, theoretical advancement, scholarly influence, and methodological originality.
```

### Phase-based subsections

Use chronological or conceptual phases.

Each phase subsection should include:

1. Phase overview:
   - date range or developmental period
   - number/type of works if available
   - why the phase matters
2. Foundational contributions:
   - identify what these works introduced
   - explain their theoretical/methodological move
3. Thematic consolidation:
   - group works by shared concern
   - avoid article-by-article summary when possible
4. Transition:
   - explain how the phase leads to the next phase

Template phase headings:

```text
4.1 [Seminal/theoretical foundations of the field]
4.2 [Maturity, consolidation, and core debates]
4.3 [Emerging topics and new empirical domains]
4.4 [Innovative methodologies and future-facing applications]
```

### Within-phase paragraph roles

Use these paragraph functions:

- phase framing paragraph
- theory lineage paragraph
- key contribution cluster
- empirical application cluster
- tension or limitation paragraph
- transition paragraph

## 5. Discussion and Conclusions

Approximate function: turn the review into a research agenda.

### Opening discussion paragraph

Role:

- state what the review reveals overall
- identify the main conceptual trajectory
- reconnect to review purpose

### Future research agenda paragraphs

Use 2-4 agenda directions.

Each agenda direction should:

1. name the future research area
2. explain why it follows from the review
3. specify what future studies should examine
4. explain the theoretical/practical stakes

Possible agenda categories:

- new technologies and methods
- institutional/cultural/political context
- emerging empirical settings
- methodological innovation
- concept integrity and risk of dilution

### Closing paragraph

Role:

- end with a conceptual warning or strong framing
- explain what must be protected as the field develops

Template:

```text
Future research should interpret [concept] according to its operational contexts to avoid the risk that it becomes merely [technique/legitimation device], thereby denying its critical nature.
```

## References

Function:

- show theoretical foundations
- show reviewed corpus
- support method choices

## Appendix

Function:

- hold supplementary corpus details
- keep main text readable
- support transparency and replicability

## Writing Style Rules

- Use formal but direct review language.
- Combine systematic method with interpretive narrative.
- Do not let bibliometric tables replace conceptual discussion.
- Use phases to make a large literature readable.
- Use the discussion to produce an agenda, not just a summary.
- Distinguish "what has been done" from "what could be done."
