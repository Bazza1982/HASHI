# Mini Test Write From AAAJ SLR Template v2

Purpose: test whether the template can guide a new SLR-style opening without
copying the source article topic or wording.

Test topic: AI assurance in public-sector accountability.

## 1. Introduction Mini Draft

### P1 Field emergence

AI assurance has become an increasingly visible concern in public-sector
accountability research as governments adopt automated decision systems,
predictive analytics, and generative AI tools in service delivery, audit, and
regulation. The growth of this literature reflects a broader concern that
algorithmic systems are no longer peripheral technical instruments, but
institutional arrangements that shape how public decisions are justified,
reviewed, and contested.

Template role check: field named, timeliness established, significance linked
to disciplinary concern.

### P2 Practice/concept rationale

The practical difficulty is that public agencies are expected to use AI systems
efficiently while also demonstrating transparency, fairness, explainability, and
legal defensibility. This creates an accountability problem: assurance work must
translate technical system behaviour into evidence that citizens, regulators,
auditors, and public managers can understand and challenge.

Template role check: concrete governance problem stated; rationale is not just
technological.

### P3 Representative approaches

Existing studies address this problem through several approaches, including
algorithmic audit, model documentation, risk assessment, impact assessment,
human oversight, procurement governance, and post-deployment monitoring. These
approaches share a concern with making AI systems governable, but they differ
in whether assurance is treated as technical verification, organisational
control, legal compliance, or democratic accountability.

Template role check: representative approaches listed compactly; paragraph
does not over-review.

### P4 Main contrast

This review distinguishes AI assurance from narrower forms of technical
validation. Whereas technical validation asks whether a system performs as
specified, public-sector AI assurance asks whether the system can be justified
within institutional expectations of accountability, rights protection, and
public trust.

Template role check: contrast clarifies conceptual stakes.

### P5 Positive definition

Understood in this broader sense, AI assurance enables researchers to examine
how evidence, expertise, governance arrangements, and public values are brought
together to support or challenge automated public decisions. It therefore opens
space for analysing assurance as a social and institutional practice, rather
than as a purely technical checklist.

Template role check: positive definition explains what the concept makes
possible.

### P6 Review questions

This review asks: how has AI assurance been conceptualised in public-sector
accountability research; what methods and institutional settings have been
examined; and what future research is needed to connect technical assurance
with democratic accountability?

Template role check: questions convert field problem into review agenda.

### P7 Review purposes

The review has three purposes. First, it maps the scope and characteristics of
the emerging literature. Second, it synthesises how AI assurance is defined,
operationalised, and evaluated across public-sector contexts. Third, it
develops a research agenda for studying assurance as an accountability practice
that connects technical evidence with public justification.

Template role check: purposes align with later method, synthesis, and agenda
sections.

### P8 Roadmap

The article proceeds as follows. The next section outlines the theoretical
antecedents of AI assurance and public-sector accountability. The review design
then explains the search strategy, inclusion criteria, and analytical methods.
The exploratory analysis maps publication trends, methods, sectors, and
research clusters. The narrative review synthesises the development of the
field across key themes, before the final section develops a research agenda
and discusses the risks of treating assurance as a procedural substitute for
accountability.

Template role check: roadmap previews the whole article and ends with the
conceptual warning direction.

## Self-Evaluation

Strengths:

- The template successfully forced an eight-paragraph introduction rather than
  a generic one-paragraph summary.
- Each paragraph has a clear job and a transition into the next.
- The test topic changed completely from the source PDF while preserving the
  rhetorical structure.
- The final paragraph already creates a bridge to method, synthesis, agenda,
  and conceptual warning.

Weaknesses:

- A real article would still need topic-specific evidence before the method and
  review sections can be drafted.
- The template guides structure well, but it should still be paired with a
  source-corpus reading step before producing claims about a real literature.

Decision:

This mini test supports handing v2 to Barry for review as a candidate-stable
EXP artifact. It is not yet globally stable for all journals; it is stable for
the specific task of extracting an AAAJ-style SLR writing template from a
published source article after a deep-reading gate.
