# Briefing Paper Writing Playbook

Use this playbook when Barry asks for a real Audit Office briefing paper after
the format EXP is validated.

## Writing Pattern

1. Start with the briefing metadata table:
   - To
   - Date
   - Ref
   - Subject
   - Prepared by
   - Presented by
2. Write `Purpose` as a short executive paragraph.
3. Explain what is proposed and why it matters.
4. Summarise executive feedback or stakeholder feedback.
5. Define what is in scope.
6. Define what is out of scope.
7. Connect the proposal to strategies, governance, or operating priorities.
8. Identify governance, project team, owners, and advisors.

## Language Style

- Write in concise executive briefing language.
- Use risk-aware, decision-oriented phrasing.
- Avoid promotional language.
- Avoid over-technical implementation detail unless it affects a decision.
- Separate exploration, design, testing, governance, and implementation.
- Frame AI/data proposals as controlled, auditable, human-in-the-loop, and
  aligned with existing strategy.

## Do Not

- Do not copy source content.
- Do not invent stakeholder positions.
- Do not treat exploratory proposals as approved implementation.
- Do not let ethics/workforce risk dominate unless Barry asks.
