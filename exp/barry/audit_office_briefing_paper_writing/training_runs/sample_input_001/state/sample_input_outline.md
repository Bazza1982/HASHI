Briefing note

OFFICIAL

To: Data and AI Governance GroupFrom: Data TeamSubject: Data Platform – Data retention and lifecycle managementDate: May 2026

Prepared by:

Nathan Carter, Director, Accounting, Technical and Data

Barry Li, Associate Director, Data

Purpose

The purpose of this briefing note is to seek endorsement of a revised data retention practice for the Audit Office data platform, and to outline how this proposal aligns with broader information lifecycle management objectives, including work being progressed under the Data Zen project.

Specifically, this paper recommends adopting a standard fiveyear data retention period for the data platform, replacing the current practice of routinely deleting data after one year. The proposal is intended to improve the Audit Office’s ability to perform longterm audit and analytical work while remaining compliant with legislative, privacy and information management obligations.

Background and context

Overview of the data platform

The Audit Office data platform is a secure, endtoend storage and analytics environment used to support digital audit and analytics activities across the state and local government sectors.

The platform enables:

automated ingestion of standardised datasets used in audits;

secure storage of raw and curated audit data;

analytics and visualisation to support audit risk assessment, testing and insights; and

controlled access for authorised Data Team and audit staff.

The platform is a core enabler of the Audit Office’s digital audit strategy and is intended to support more continuous, consistent and datadriven audit approaches.

How the platform operates

Data is extracted from agency systems (primarily via shared service providers), transferred securely into the platform, and processed through automated pipelines. Data is stored and analysed within a segregated environment, with access restricted to approved users and governed by established security, privacy and information handling controls.

The platform has been designed to reduce manual handling of data, improve consistency in audit analytics, and enable reuse of data across audit cycles where appropriate.

Current data retention practice

Under current practice, most datasets ingested into the data platform are retained for a short period (generally up to one year) before being deleted and rerequested in subsequent audit cycles.

While this approach has minimised data holdings, it has resulted in several limitations:

limited ability to perform longitudinal analysis across multiple audit cycles;

reduced capacity to identify trends, patterns and emerging risks over time;

constraints on benchmarking and sectorwide analytics; and

repeated data acquisition effort for both the Audit Office and auditees.

As the platform matures, these limitations increasingly constrain the value that can be derived from data already collected for audit purposes.

Legal, policy and privacy considerations

Legislative authority and audit use

The Audit Office collects and holds data under its legislative audit mandate. Data stored on the data platform is subject to the same secrecy, confidentiality and use restrictions as audit data held through other means.

Use of the data platform does not expand or alter the Audit Office’s authority to collect or use information. Data held on the platform is restricted to audit, assurance and approved analytical purposes only.

Privacy and personal information

Some datasets ingested into the data platform include personal information, particularly payroll and user access data. The Audit Office applies a riskbased approach to managing this information, including:

limiting collection to data required for audit purposes;

excluding or transiently processing particularly sensitive fields;

applying encryption and deidentification where appropriate; and

enforcing strict access controls and monitoring.

Extending the retention period does not change the nature of the data collected or weaken existing privacy controls.

Records management

Audit data is required to be retained in accordance with records management obligations. Establishing a defined retention period for data held on the platform supports clearer, more consistent and more defensible lifecycle management decisions.

Industry and public sector practice

In public sector audit, finance and assurance functions, multiyear retention of financial and transactional data is common practice. Retaining data over several years enables:

analysis of trends and movements over time;

identification of systemic issues and recurring control weaknesses;

benchmarking across entities and sectors; and

more informed insights for executive, governance and parliamentary reporting.

A oneyear retention window materially limits these capabilities.

Proposed data retention approach

It is proposed that the Audit Office adopt a standard fiveyear retention period for datasets stored on the data platform, subject to the following principles:

Scope: Applies to standard audit datasets ingested into the platform, including financial, payroll and user access data.

Purpose limitation: Data continues to be used strictly for audit, assurance and approved analytics purposes.

Privacy and security: Existing privacy, encryption, deidentification and access control measures remain in place.

Governance: Retention and disposal are managed in accordance with approved policies and records management requirements.

Disposal: Data older than five years is securely destroyed unless subject to a legal hold or specific retention obligation.

Alignment with the Data Zen project

One of the objectives of the Data Zen project is to establish clearer and more consistent lifecycle management for content held in Audit Office systems, including how information is created, managed, retained and disposed of.

While this briefing focuses on the data platform, the proposed fiveyear retention period aligns with the intent of Data Zen by:

introducing clearer lifecycle definitions for analytical and audit data; and

reducing reliance on informal or ad hoc retention practices.

This proposal represents an early, practical step toward more consistent lifecycle management across systems, without preempting broader Data Zen outcomes.

Nuix – lifecycle management considerations

Nuix is one of the systems in scope for Data Zen and is used to support auditrelated activities involving the ingestion, processing and analysis of content.

As Business User and Business System Owner of Nuix, the Data Team intends to commence structured lifecycle management work for this system. The purpose of this work is to:

discuss the current use of Nuix, including how content is created, managed and used;

understand existing processes, practices and controls around the system;

clarify ownership roles and responsibilities; and

establish a clear and agreed approach to managing the lifecycle of content held in Nuix.

This work will be progressed separately but in alignment with the data platform retention decision, to promote consistency in lifecycle management principles across Audit Office systems.

Roles, responsibilities and governance

Effective lifecycle management requires clarity around:

system ownership;

responsibility for managing retention and disposal; and

oversight of compliance with approved retention practices.

The proposed retention policy for the data platform does not alter existing accountability arrangements. Instead, it provides a clear and documented baseline that can inform similar work across other systems, including Nuix.

Consideration of DAChosted environments

The data platform is hosted by the Data Analytics Centre (DAC). While DAC provides the technical environment, the Audit Office remains responsible for decisions relating to:

what data is retained;

how long it is retained for; and

when data is disposed of.

As lifecycle management work progresses, consideration should be given to whether similar lifecycle documentation and governance approaches should be formally articulated for other DAChosted environments used by the Audit Office, to ensure consistency and clarity across arrangements.

Benefits of the proposed approach

Adopting a fiveyear retention period would:

enable longterm financial and audit analytics, including analysis of trends in government spending and payroll;

support sectorwide benchmarking and comparative analysis;

enhance audit quality by allowing currentyear results to be assessed in historical context;

reduce repeated data acquisition effort and associated operational risk; and

strengthen the return on investment in the data platform by maximising the analytical value of data already collected.

Risks and mitigations

Recommendation

That the Data and AI Governance Group:

Endorse the adoption of a standard fiveyear data retention policy for the Audit Office data platform; and

Note the alignment of this approach with broader lifecycle management work being progressed under the Data Zen project, including future consideration of systems such as Nuix and DAChosted environments.
