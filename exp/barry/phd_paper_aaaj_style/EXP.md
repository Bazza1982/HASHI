# Barry PhD Paper AAAJ Style EXP

Status: stable for the specific Paper 1 AAAJ draft editing/rebuild context

This EXP stores training material and validated procedure for Barry's PhD Paper
1 AAAJ-style Word document. It is context-specific and should not be treated as
a general academic writing skill.

## Purpose

Learn how Barry writes, structures, and formats this specific paper style:

- PhD Paper 1 systematic literature review draft
- AAAJ journal template preferences
- supervisor-preferred style and formatting
- Barry's own wording, section structure, and revision pattern

## Training material

The copied source material is stored in:

```text
training_materials/with_new_template_paper_1_slr_aaaj_jan_2026
```

The directory contains multiple Word draft versions and drafting notes. These
should be treated as examples, not as generic templates for every paper.

## How to train this EXP

1. Inspect the Word documents and identify stable style features.
2. Separate journal/template requirements from Barry-specific writing habits.
3. Separate supervisor preferences from generic academic conventions.
4. Record formatting rules only after checking multiple versions.
5. Build validators for Word structure, headings, references, tables, captions,
   and layout once the style has been extracted.
6. Promote this EXP from candidate only after it can reproduce the style on a
   new practice section and pass validation.

## Early assumptions to validate

- AAAJ template formatting matters.
- Supervisor preferences may override generic journal formatting instincts.
- Barry's draft versions reveal both final style and revision trajectory.
- The most recent draft is likely the strongest example, but earlier versions
  may explain why the style changed.

## Scope limit

This EXP is stable for editing and rebuilding this specific AAAJ-style PhD Paper
1 draft from scratch in Word. It does not automatically apply to other journals,
other drafts, or later templates without a new training run.

## Stable procedure

For exact draft editing/rebuild tasks:

1. Start from a new Word document inside the source draft's template context.
2. Clear the new document content.
3. Rebuild or insert the edited content into the empty document.
4. Export source and rebuilt documents to PDF.
5. Render and compare every page when exact visual preservation is required.
6. Do not accept page count alone as proof of success.
